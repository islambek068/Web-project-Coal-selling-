Coal

Coal (fossil coal) is a sedimentary rock, a mineral, the most valuable type of fuel and raw material for the chemical, and not only, industry. The international name for carbon comes from lat. carbō - "coal". Fossil coal was formed from spores, parts of the epidermis of pluns, ferns and other ancient plants (350-250 million years ago) - lean coals, anthracites, coking, low-caking, gas, long-flame, and other subspecies of coal. Then there was a carbonless period, then brown coals, sapropels, peats were formed under the influence of high temperatures and without access to oxygen, and earlier, until the carbonless period, without the presence of microorganisms processing wood residues.

Anthracite

Descrption

The best grade of coal, characterized by black color, strong luster, high calorific value. The highest kind of coal is a solid combustible mineral formed from plant residues as a result of humification and carbonification

Сhemical composition:

  • C=94-98%
  • N+O=1-3%
  • H=1-3%

Coal

Description

According to its chemical composition, coal is a mixture of high-molecular polycyclic aromatic compounds with a high mass fraction of carbon, as well as water and volatile substances with small amounts of mineral impurities that form ash when burning coal. Fossil coals differ from each other in the ratio of their constituent components, which determines their heat of combustion.

Сhemical composition:

  • C=78-90%
  • N+O=19-4%
  • H=4-6%

Brown coal

Descrption

They are formed from dead organic residues under load pressure and under the influence of elevated temperature at depths of about one kilometer.

Chemical composition:

  • C=60-75%
  • N+O=34-17%
  • H=4-8%

Charcoal

Descrption

The finished coal consists mainly of carbon. The advantage of using charcoal instead of conventional wood burning is the absence of water and other components. This allows charcoal to burn at higher temperatures and emit very little smokeю.

Chemical composition:

  • C=80-92%
  • N+O=5-15%
  • H=4-5%
